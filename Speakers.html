Speakers in the Event

Dr. Ashish Goel, DG NRIDA

Dr. Ashish Kumar Goel joined the Indian Administrative Service in 1995. Before joining in the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India, he has had extensive experience of working in the Government of Uttar Pradesh in various key positions. Recently, he was entrusted with the task of organizing Prayagraj Kumbh, 2019 in his role as Divisional Commissioner, Prayagraj. Kumbh, 2019 has been hailed as the first ever Open-defecation free, Odour free and Garbage free Kumbh, and also an incident free Kumbh ever. Earlier, he has held positions such as MD, UP Road Transport Corporation; MD, West UP Power Distribution Corporation; Secretary, Basic Education; and Secretary, Rural Development in the State Government. He has been Collector/ District Magistrate in many districts of UP such as Prayagraj (Allahabad), Agra, Bareilly, Kanpur (Rural), Sultanpur, Muzaffarnagar, etc. Dr. Goel is a graduate in Electrical Engineering from IIT, Delhi. During the course of his service, he has also acquired M.A. and Ph.D. in Economics. He also attended M.Sc. programme in Public Management and Governance at the London School of Economics and Political Science, London, UK.

PIARC Dr. Phill Paige Green, South Africa

Phil graduated with a BSc (Hons) and MSc from the University of Natal and a PhD from the University of Pretoria, 1989, all in Engineering Geology. He worked at the Council for Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR) in Pretoria South Africa (and for 2 years in Muscat, Sultanate of Oman), for 37 years in 27 countries. ADB/World Bank

The World Bank is an international financial institution that provides loans and grants to the governments of low- and middle-income countries for the purpose of pursuing capital projects. The World Bank is the collective name for the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD) and the International Development Association (IDA), two of five international organizations owned by the World Bank Group.The World Bank is run by a president and 25 executive directors, as well as 29 various vice presidents. IBRD and IDA have 189 and 174 member countries, respectively. The U.S., Japan, China, Germany and the U.K. have the most voting power. The bank aims loans at developing countries to help reduce poverty. The bank is engaged in several global partnerships and initiatives, and takes a role in working toward addressing climate change.
